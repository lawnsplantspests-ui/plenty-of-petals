How it works

From your vision to a box of blooms.

  1. Tell us your vision. Share your colors, flower preferences, and roughly how many arrangements you need. Inspiration photos are great if you have them.
  2. We build your order. We recommend quantities (so you don't over- or under-buy) and source everything fresh. No guesswork on your end.
  3. Pick up & arrange. Your blooms are ready for pickup at our studio a day or two before your event, hydrated and prepped so you can get straight to creating.

FAQ

Common questions about DIY.

Do I need to be a florist to order?

Not at all — our DIY flowers are made for brides, families, and anyone who wants gorgeous blooms without the full-service price. If you can follow a YouTube tutorial and care enough to do it well, you can DIY a beautiful bridal party.

Will the flowers be ready to use?

Yes. They arrive fresh, hydrated, and prepped so you can start arranging right away. We strip lower foliage and cut stems where it matters. The hardest prep is already done before pickup.

Can you help me figure out how much I need?

Absolutely — that's part of the service. Just tell us your vision (number of arrangements, sizes, types of pieces) and we'll recommend quantities. We'd rather steer you to the right amount than have you stuck with too many or too few.

How far in advance should I order?

For weddings: at least 4–6 weeks. Some specialty blooms have to be sourced in advance, and we want to lock in availability. Smaller orders for showers or rehearsal dinners can sometimes be put together with a couple weeks' notice — ask us.

What flowers are available?

It depends on the season and your colors. Roses, hydrangeas, eucalyptus, ranunculus, garden roses, dahlias, peonies (in season), tulips, lisianthus, and more — we source from professional wholesalers and can usually get whatever you need with enough lead time.

Do you teach DIY arranging?

We don't run formal classes, but if you're picking up a bulk order we're happy to walk you through technique basics when you arrive. For more in-depth guidance, YouTube has hours of great content from professional florists — we can point you to the best ones for your vibe.

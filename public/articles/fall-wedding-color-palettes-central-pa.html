Seasonal inspiration

Fall wedding color palettes.

Fall is Central PA's most photogenic wedding season — golden light, turning leaves, and a flower market full of rich color. Here are five palettes we love designing, and the real flowers that deliver each one.

← All planning articles

1. Terracotta, rust & cream

The defining fall palette of the last few years, and it's earned it — warm without being orange, rustic without being country. It glows against barn wood and photographs beautifully in golden-hour light.

  • The flowers: toffee and terracotta roses, dahlias in burnt tones, marigolds, dried grasses and bunny tail, cream lisianthus for softness
  • Best for: barn and farm venues, outdoor ceremonies in the landscape

2. Burgundy, blush & deep green

The classic that never misses. Burgundy brings the drama, blush keeps it romantic, and deep greenery ties it to the season. It works formal or rustic — one of the few palettes equally at home in a ballroom and a barn.

  • The flowers: burgundy dahlias and garden roses, blush spray roses, hydrangea, dark foliage like leucadendron or copper beech
  • Best for: almost anywhere — this palette adapts to the venue

3. Mustard, sage & ivory

Softer and more unexpected than the classic fall oranges. Mustard yellow reads warm and vintage, sage keeps it calm, and ivory gives the eye somewhere to rest. Gorgeous with velvet ribbon and brass accents.

  • The flowers: golden celosia, yarrow, sunflower varieties with soft centers, sage-toned eucalyptus, ivory roses and stock
  • Best for: historic venues, restaurant receptions, early-fall dates

4. Plum, dusty rose & smoke

Moody, romantic, and made for late-fall light. Deep purples and muted rose tones feel rich without the red-orange spectrum every other fall wedding uses — a favorite for November couples.

  • The flowers: plum dahlias, purple carnations (trust us — modern varieties are stunning), dusty rose lisianthus, smoke bush foliage, silver-toned greens
  • Best for: candlelit receptions, ballrooms, late-season weddings

5. Copper, chocolate & champagne

The palette for couples who want warm neutrals instead of color — tones of caramel, bronze, and cream that make skin glow in photos and let textures (velvet, dried elements, candlelight) do the talking.

  • The flowers: chocolate cosmos, bronze mums, caramel roses, dried florals and grasses, champagne-toned lisianthus
  • Best for: intimate weddings, neutral-toned venues, couples who love a dried-floral look
A note on what's actually in season: fall is dahlia season in Pennsylvania — the best month of the year for them — along with mums, celosia, zinnias (early fall), and spectacular dried elements. Our month-by-month flower guide covers what's at its peak for your specific date.
